Ghost Rider

Ghost Rider is a fictional superhero appearing in American comic books published by Marvel Comics. Created by writer Gary Friedrich and artist Mike Ploog, Ghost Rider first appeared in Marvel Spotlight #5 in 1972. The character is known for his distinctive appearance: a flaming skull for a head, a leather jacket, and a powerful motorcycle. The mantle of Ghost Rider has been taken up by different characters over the years, the most notable being Johnny Blaze and Danny Ketch. Johnny Blaze, a stunt motorcyclist, becomes the Ghost Rider after making a pact with the demon Mephisto to save his stepfather's life, only to be cursed to transform into the skeletal, flame-headed Spirit of Vengeance. As Ghost Rider, he possesses supernatural powers, including superhuman strength, durability, and the ability to project and control hellfire. He uses these abilities to fight evil and seek vengeance on wrongdoers, often grappling with the demonic entity within him. Ghost Rider has become a popular character in the Marvel Universe, known for his dark, gothic aesthetic and his complex, morally ambiguous nature.
